ARTIST STATEMENT

Isabella G. Sziraczky (b. 1996) is a visual artist from the San Francisco Bay Area. Born to Hungarian parents, she is influenced by Eastern European art and culture. She incorporates Hungarian language and traditional embroidery aesthetics into her work. Sziraczky specializes in oil painting, printmaking, drawing, and collage. She is currently finishing up her Bachelor of Fine Arts degree with a focus in two-dimensional art at San Jose State University and plans on obtaining a Master's Degree in Art Therapy in the future. Her creative process is greatly inspired by music. She played classical piano and danced while growing up. Being active in performing arts allowed her to express herself creatively and discover her passion for visual art. She creates artwork through a process of self-reflection and emotional processing. Fascinated by color interactions, she explores how colors influence our emotions and visual perceptioin. Being able to depict her insights allows viewers the opportunity to observe her emotions affiliated with certain ecperiences. She combines elements of surrealistic figures with abstracted spaces and forms to challenge perceptions of reality. Many of her compostitions and designs are inspired by organic materials and natural structures found throughout Northern California, particularly marine and forest environments. She enjoys experimenting with non-traditional materials and processes to create collages, paintings, and prints. She strives to create artistic pieces that reflect on the highs and lows of my experiences throuh early adulthood.
